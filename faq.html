General Information about the process

The refugee process in Switzerland is quite difficult to understand. That’s why we wrote this to help Ukrainian refugees to help to get through this process in Switzerland. You will need 4 Steps which you have to go through to get the S-Status the accommodation and if you have financial problems to get social help from government.

1. Registration in Federal Asylum Centre (FAC | BAZ) and applying S-Status

This is the first step to take, when you have decided to flee to Switzerland. You need to apply for S-Status. The status can be applied in one of the six FAC processing facilities.

For this process you have two possibilities:

The first approach is to use RegisterMe and fill out the form. You should retrieve an appointment in such an FAC facility. Follow up this appointment and then follow the instructions of the person, which is responsible for you.

The second possibility is to go directly to one of the six FACs and apply onsite for the S-Status. It is not recommended to use this approach, as the queue time for the entrance is rather long. Hence, the processing in FAC will be faster by using RegisterMe.

2. Go to your accommodation

After arriving, there are two possibilities for accommodation in Switzerland. In case an accommodation has already been occupied, one can back to the accommodation and wait for the decision of the FAC if the S-Status is granted or not.

In case that one does not have an accommodation, the FAC will help to get one. If this is not possible one will have the possibility to stay in the FAC itself. In both of those cases the person must wait for the confirmation of the FAC that the S-Status request had been processed and granted or rejected.

3. Go to migration centre

When you have been granted S-Status, you will receive a confirmation by letter that you have been recognised as S-Status. You will then receive an appointment to go to the migration centre. Your documents for S-Status will then be drawn up there.

4. Go to the district office (Gemeinde or Kreisbüro)

After the previous process, you have to go to the district office to get a confirmation of residence. At this place (your place of residence) you can then apply for social assistance.

Social assistance is a temporary monetary aid. You receive money from the state every month. However, this is only possible if you have financial problems. The district office will tell you everything you need to know about this and how to apply for this assistance.

In addition, health insurance is compulsory in Switzerland. To obtain health insurance, it is also advisable to ask at the district office, as you can be helped quickly and easily at this location.
